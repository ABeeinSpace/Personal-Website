The world runs on the Internet. This very project is hosted on the Internet!! The transcript that I wrote up for the project is getting saved to my OneDrive (Microsoft’s answer to Google Drive). The HTML code is getting hosted on a Git repository in the cloud. Soon, the IoT (Internet of Things) will take over, allowing us to turn the kitchen light off from our smartphones while at the office. Or, start the oven preheating as one starts their commute (regardless of the fire risk this poses. There is a reason why one is not supposed to leave the oven unattended). I can reboot my router from my phone regardless of where I’m at. I could be in Orlando or Tampa and troubleshoot a misbehaving Internet connection through an app.

However, all of this connectivity does not come without risks. One shudders to imagine what would happen if someone managed to compromise a company like Google. Hundreds of thousands of other companies, schools, and even individuals run off of Google services. There would be absolute pandemonium! Educational institutions (including SBHC) would shed Chromebooks like they are hot potatoes and move away from G Suite as fast as possible!

Or if Microsoft was compromised and threat actors (bad guys) served malware through Windows Update. The bad guys would instantly have access to over a billion computers. They could create a super-botnet that could take down any company. Not even the Big Three (Google, Apple, Microsoft) would be immune to such an attack.

Apple has copies of messages stored on their servers because of its iMessage service. However, an attack on Apple would not be game over, as the company uses super-strong encryption that not even they can break.
