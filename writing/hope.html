Hope is a tenacious thing, clinging to life for all its worth and it never really goes away. That's what’s fascinating about it, it never truly dies, it simply gets buried under all the crap and cruft of modern life. If one looks hard enough, looks for a glimmer of golden light in a sea of drab grey darkness, then it’s still there. Just as bright as day one. Hope is enough to turn even the most cynical of pessimists into optimists given enough of it and enough time. For some of us, it's always been there in truly massive quantities and that’s called optimism. Some of us have a ridiculous amount and so we see the good in everything eventually whether we want to or not.

It’s refreshing to see someone in his thirties who is seemingly a twelve-year old trapped in the body of a thirty year old married guy. He sees the fun and adventure in everything, he’s a millionaire, he’s got it made. And he’s happier than most of us in our teen years. That says something. Of course, I’m talking about my bouncy boi Brendon Urie, of Panic! At The Disco fame. For one of his music videos (High Hopes for those wondering) he climbs up a building! Granted, part of the video is faked for insurance reasons, but that’s the only reason it was faked! Brendon was doing flips off of the equipment! He’s my bouncing optimistic boi and he’s great!

Some of my optimism is probably showing through in this post, but guess what? I do not care in the slightest and there is a reason I do not care. I’m a computer ninja who likes screaming and running around in circles because I can! I’m hopeful for the future and want to see what it brings because im an optimist damnit!
